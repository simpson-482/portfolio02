Relationship Between Gods and Divinities-- Classical Mythology-- Rewrite

I believe that generally the Gods are concerned with their own self-interest while mostly choosing to favor or not favor humans based on the human's extraordinary disposition, the God’s desire for esteem, grudges and lineage. Athene and Hera take the Achaians side in the Trojan War because of Paris choosing Aphrodite as the most beautiful God you see that the Gods place emphasis on esteem and it may be a cause for favorability. Circe and Kalypso favor Odysseus because they give him sexual pleasure and they wish to keep him for their own. The Gods Zeus and Athene seem to favor Odysseus because he is a human with extraordinary ability. The favorability that is shown with Odysseus from the Gods on many occasions leads you believe that the disposition and plight of non-divinities is partially to be the cause of favorability with the Gods as Odysseus is a very noble human with an incredible back story. However, many of Odysseus’s crew gets killed with the only outcry at the time being that of Odysseus, you eventually see that the Gods also have firm lines between the will of extraordinary humans and the violation of sacred artifacts.

The Gods also seem to uphold what is right when they don’t necessarily have a favorite. When Achilles is defiling Hektor’s body the god Apollo protects it and makes sure it eventually sees a proper burial. This is one example that shows you that the Gods are concerned their own self-interest but also are concerned with the dignity and righteousness of the humans for which they govern. Along this story line you see Athene and Zeus supporting Odysseus because of his extraordinary disposition and despite there the fact that it seems like there is less in it for them.

It seems like the best way to get a meaningful divinity on your side is to have a divinity in your lineage. This seems to be somewhat of trump card when it comes to having the ultimately winning relationships of divinities on your side. You see this in both the cases of Thetis and Achilles and Poseidon and Polyphemos. Although there are other Gods taking sides during these plights, these relationships with the Gods with seem to be the most crucial. Achilles relationship with Thetis (along with the prophecy that he will die if fights with the Achains) seem to be swaying the war in favor of the Trojans. Polyphemos’s relationship with Poseidon is what prevents Odysseus from returning home with his men. The Aiolos conclude that the only way this could’ve gone wrong for the Odysseus’ crew is that the Gods are looking at the Ithacans unfavorably. Through this experience, you can conclude that the curses from the Gods can be a determining factor in the plight of the epic heroes.

The plight of characters is also important to win favorability with the Gods. Athene intervenes multiple times to save her favorite non-divinities. First, she intervenes to save Agamemnon from Achilles when she promises him he will be rewarded for letting Agamemnon live. Secondly, she intervenes to save Odysseus from certain death at the hands of Poseidon and again when he runs into trouble with the suitors. You also see divinities strike vengeance on those that disobey their warnings prophecies or messages. You see this when Aigisthos is warned by Hermes not to have an affair with Agamemnon’s wife, yet does so anyway and when Odysseus’ men eat the Helios’ sacred cattle. In both cases, death of the characters that violate these warnings is justifiable by the Gods. Justifiable death in the eyes of the Gods is something that you see semi often. Athene (still supporting Odysseus) encourages and even demands that Odysseus kill the suitors that intend to marry his wife Penelope. When you fool around with a hero’s wife, when you violate something sacred, and when the opposition has a divinity in their lineage are the seeming surefire ways of getting a divinity to hold a grudge against you.
